Communication Models
Johnson's Model (1953)
Emphasizes context and events as stimuli for communication
Highlights stages involved in the communication act
Newcomb's Model (1956)
Focuses on feedback's importance in communication
Suggests its impact on the effectiveness of message transmission
Jakobson's Model (1956)
Combines linear and triangular models
Identifies six communication functions associated with different dimensions of the communication process
Berlo's Model (1960)
Based on source, message, channel, receiver, and feedback
Illustrates the communication process from encoding to decoding of messages

Interpersonal Communication
Involves message exchange between people aiming to create shared meaning
Includes verbal and non-verbal cues, influenced by personal needs, goals, and social contexts
Serves various purposes including self-presentation, relationship development, and goal achievement
Small Group Communication
Occurs within task groups with common goals
Effective group communication enhances group functioning
Resembles interaction in society, involves sharing meaning to achieve personal and group goals
Public Speaking
Involves sharing ideas with an audience primarily through speech
Aimed at influencing audience perceptions and achieving communication goals
Requires interaction with the audience and may induce nervousness but emphasizes the audience's centrality
Models of Communication
Various models including linear, interactional, and transactional
Models like Shannon-Weaver's emphasize information transmission, while Jakobson's combines linear and triangular concepts
Transactional models stress mutual involvement and negotiation of meaning
Non-verbal Communication
Encompasses human behaviors, gestures, and expressions
Functions in regulating interaction and conveying emotions and intentions
Integral part of communication, often complements verbal communication
Facilitating Effective Communication
Cooperative learning, clear group goals, and teacher guidance enhance classroom communication
Teachers' skills in managing disruptions and maintaining engagement contribute to successful communication and learning
